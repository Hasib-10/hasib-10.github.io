About

I am interested in statistical methodology and its application to real-world data problems. My work aims to connect theoretical development with practical inference, especially in settings involving selection bias, complex sampling, and incomplete model assumptions.

My broader interests include Bayesian inference, semiparametric methods, survey statistics, and computational approaches for modern data analysis.

Thesis

On Bayesian Empirical Likelihood-based Method for Complex Survey Data with Application to Non-probability Sampling

My thesis develops a Bayesian empirical likelihood (BEL) framework for inference under complex survey designs and extends it to non-probability sampling.
