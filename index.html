PhD Student in Statistics, University of Nebraska–Lincoln

I am a graduate student in Statistics at the University of Nebraska–Lincoln. My research focuses on Bayesian empirical likelihood, survey inference, and robust statistical methods for complex survey and non-probability data.

I am advised by Dr. Sanjay Chaudhuri, and my thesis, Bayesian Empirical Likelihood-Based Method for Complex Survey Data with Application to Non-Probability Sampling, studies methods for inference under selection bias and complex sampling settings.

About

My interests include Bayesian methods, empirical likelihood, semiparametric inference, survey statistics, and computational approaches for modern data analysis. I am particularly interested in developing methods that remain reliable when standard assumptions such as probability sampling or correct model specification do not fully hold.

Beyond methodology, I enjoy using statistical tools to address applied data problems through simulation, reproducible workflows, and clear technical communication.

Research

Master’s Thesis

University of Nebraska–Lincoln · Advisor: Dr. Sanjay Chaudhuri

Bayesian Empirical Likelihood-Based Method for Complex Survey Data with Application to Non-Probability Sampling

My thesis develops Bayesian extensions of empirical likelihood for complex survey designs and non-probability samples. The work includes methodological development, simulation studies in R, and applications to real-world data.
